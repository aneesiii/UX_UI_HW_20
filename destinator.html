Project Overview

Destinator is an app for travelers to use post-pandemic to make choosing and planning a vacation a breeze.

MY ROLE: UX/UI Designer (Individual Project)

TOOLS: Figma, Miro, Invision, Google Docs, Slides, Sheets, Forms

TIMELINE: 4 Weeks


The Challenge

For my first UX design boot camp challence our cohort was tasked with designing an app for travellers to use post-pandemic


The Problem

One of the hardest parts about planning a trip is picking a destination and finding activities you would enjoy doing


The Solution

Make travellers lives easier by assisting them with finding the perfect destination along with fun activities to do that pertain to their interests





Definition and Ideation Phase

To learn more about what pain points travellers face when planning a trip, a google survey was conducted to recieive quanititative data as well as six user interviews to better understand their struggles.


Interview Key Takeaways


"Traveling can get repetitive such as sightseeing and visiting landmark, I'd like to do something different and have more variety"



"The hardest part of traveling is finding the best place to go to that will have things to entertain you. thats what majority of the planning goes into"



"Someones review will have a great factor on my decisions. if i have no idea what to expect, I am shooting blind hoping to aim at a target."



"I usually have my friends do the researching as I do not have the time or patience"



"I do not like researching at all, I want more apps that can find that for me"




Affinity Diagram

Using the findings from the user interviews and surveys, the next step was to create an affinity diagram to find commanalities among participants regarding what aspects in a travel app users needed most.



User Persona

I then created a User Persona based on off the user interviews and research helped pin point the general user for this travel app as well as portray needs and frustrations I am aiming to resolve

Meet Sandra Bolton, she has a passion for travelling but is indecisive when planning a trip, hates spending time researching, and wants to visit areas the average tourist would not travel to





Feature Prioritization Matrix

Using the MoSCoW Method, I then worked on prioritizing which features would be “must haves” based on user insights and research




Storyboard

The next step was to make a storyboard to explain a typical users journey and struggles of planning a vacation and how the destinator travel app resolves those pain points.




Competitive Analysis

I conducted an analysis for direct and indirect competitors to see what other travel apps were doing well and how I could make this app better. With this analysis, I was able to review opportunities and considerations needed to developing the app.




User Flow

To ideate on potential user journeys, I created a user flow chart. This was an important step to think through the potential features within the app.




Sketching and Iterations

Before creating the low-fi prototype, I sketched out the screens to think through the process of how users would interact with the app. I then testest them with users to ensure that travellers were easily able to navigate through the screens.




Mid-Fi Prototypes

The next step was to create Mid-Fi prototypes which were then tested again with users. During the testing phase, users mentioned that they wanted to be able to put in their preferences if they did not have a destination in mind. Users also wanted to be able to read a description about each activity and other reviews before deciding to book an activity.




Hi-Fi Prototype




Final Thoughts


This was my first UX/UI challenge in my boot camp using my new learnings to research and ideate an app.

Looking back, some of the changes I would make is to test the color scheme for accessibility and do more

user tests on the navigation and use of the app.

Next Steps

1. Conduct more tests and gather more participants to use the app and provide feedback on their thoughts and struggles while using the app

2. Implement a “find the perfect place to stay” feature as well

3. Work on iterating the app and ask more questions to narrow down selections to find the best destinations and activities for users
